Bishop Sedgwick Daniels passes away Board Member

Bishop Sedgwick Daniels, a prominent figure in the Church of God in Christ (COGIC) community, has passed away at the age of 67. He was widely recognized for his visionary leadership, unwavering commitment to social causes, and generous philanthropic efforts. Bishop Daniels’ impact extended far beyond his local community, leaving an indelible mark on a global scale. His passing has left a significant void within both the church and the broader community, evoking a profound mix of sorrow and gratitude for his outstanding contributions. The family of Bishop Daniels has officially announced a public memorial service to honor his enduring influence and legacy.

Who was Bishop Sedgwick Daniels? His life and career

Bishop Sedgwick Daniels was a renowned religious and social leader in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He was born on July 7, 1956, in Milwaukee and grew up in a family with a strong tradition of serving the church. From a young age, Bishop Daniels displayed leadership abilities and a deep passion for serving the Lord.

He was ordained as a minister at the age of 18 and served as an assistant pastor under the guidance of his father, the late Bishop C.H. Daniels. In 1984, he succeeded his father as the senior pastor of the Church of God in Christ in Milwaukee. From that point on, he expanded the church’s influence not only within the city but also nationally and internationally.

Leadership Roles in the Church of God in Christ

Throughout his career, Bishop Daniels played a pivotal role in the Church of God in Christ. He served as the Chairman of the Board of Bishops, a member of the General Board, and the Regional Financial Secretary for Wisconsin’s Region 1.

With a far-reaching vision, a profound understanding of the Scriptures, and captivating preaching abilities, Bishop Daniels drew followers from all around the world. He organized numerous international missionary trips to spread the Gospel and establish churches in countries such as Kenya, Ghana, India, Brazil, and many others.

Social and Philanthropic Activities

In addition to his role as a pastor, Bishop Daniels was a dedicated social activist. He tirelessly fought for justice, equality, and empowerment, particularly for the African American community.

He founded and supported several non-profit organizations, including:

1. Daniels-Mardak Youth Club for young men and women.
2. The Urban League of Milwaukee.
3. The Milwaukee Community Magazine.
4. The Council for the Advancement of Black Men in Milwaukee.

Bishop Daniels’ efforts were widely recognized through numerous prestigious awards, including the Martin Luther King Jr. Leadership Award, the NAACP Lifetime Achievement Award, and the Governor’s Pioneer Award in Wisconsin.

The Passing of Bishop Sedgwick Daniels

After a two-year battle with prostate cancer, Bishop Sedgwick Daniels peacefully passed away on November 11, 2023, at the age of 67.

This sorrowful news was announced by the Church of God in Christ (COGIC) on Facebook on Monday. In their message, the church expressed deep grief over this significant loss but encouraged the faithful to trust that with God, nothing is impossible.

Bishop Daniels’ passing has left a profound sense of mourning within both the church and the community. Many religious, political, and social leaders have expressed heartfelt condolences and deep appreciation for his substantial contributions to the community.

The family of Bishop Daniels has also announced that a public memorial service will be held on November 18 to honor his life and legacy. The memorial service will be streamed online for those unable to attend in person.

Cause of Bishop Sedgwick Daniels’ Passing

Bishop Sedgwick Daniels passed away after a two-year battle with prostate cancer.

According to the family’s statement, he was diagnosed with late-stage prostate cancer in 2021. After the diagnosis, he underwent multiple treatments and surgeries in an effort to extend his life.

However, Bishop Daniels’ health deteriorated rapidly. The pain and symptoms caused by cancer became increasingly severe. Despite these challenges, he continued to courageously fight and fulfill his ministry mission until his final days.

Multiple sources indicate that Bishop Daniels’ health took a serious turn for the worse in early November 2023. He passed away at his home on November 11, at the age of 67.

The departure of this talented and influential Bishop has left a great sense of grief for his family and congregation. Though he may not have overcome the relentless disease, his contributions to the community will always be remembered.

Obituary and Funeral Details

Following the announcement of the passing of Bishop Sedgwick Daniels, heartfelt condolences and tributes have poured in from all around.

In their public statement, Bishop Daniels’ family expressed boundless gratitude for the sincere condolences from friends, colleagues, and various organizations with whom he collaborated. They also extended heartfelt thanks to the thousands who sent their condolences, shared memories, and prayed for the family during this painful time.

Bishop Sedgwick Daniels’ funeral will be held with great solemnity at the Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception in Milwaukee at 10:00 AM on Friday, November 18, 2023. The burial service will be officiated by the Archbishop of Milwaukee, with the presence of bishops, priests from the diocese, and a large congregation.

Subsequently, the casket will be taken to the Cemetery of St. Sebastian Parish, where Bishop Daniels will rest in peace. At 11:30 AM on the same day, a prayer service for his soul will be held at the Church of God in Christ, organized by the family.
